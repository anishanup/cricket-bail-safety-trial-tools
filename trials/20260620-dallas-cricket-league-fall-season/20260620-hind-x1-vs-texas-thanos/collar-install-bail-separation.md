# Bail Guard: Learnings from a DCL Game

*Game played on June 20, 2026*

**Match:** Hind X1 vs Texas Thanos, DLCL Fall 30 Over Tournament 2026 (Match ID 5319). [Scorecard](https://www.dallascricket.org/tournament/36/view/5319)

**Short version:** The bail came off because the device was installed incorrectly.

## What happened

The ball hit the stumps and a bail popped off, like normal. But this time the
bail also came off its silicone o-ring and landed on the ground away from the
stumps. The o-ring itself was fine. It did not snap or stretch out.

When we checked the setup afterwards, we found the problem. The device had been
installed incorrectly.

## A quick look at how the device works

Each bail is held by a silicone o-ring. The o-ring is connected by a short
tether (a small leash) to a **collar** that sits on the stump. The collar sits
between two orange **stoppers**, one above it and one below it. The collar can
move a little in the gap between the two stoppers.

The design principle is that the collar can **move a little** when the
bail flies off. Think of it like catching a ball with soft hands instead of
stiff hands. You move with it for a moment so the catch is gentle. That small
bit of movement softens the tug on the o-ring, so the o-ring keeps its grip on
the bail and the bail stays attached.

## What went wrong here

Only one of the two stoppers had been fitted. The second stopper was still
unused, sitting in the bail guard packet. With just one stopper, the collar had
been pushed up tight against it and was pinned there, with no gap to move into.
With the collar jammed in place, it could not move at all.

So when the bail flew off and reached the end of its tether, there was nothing
to soften the stop. Instead of a gentle pull, the bail got a hard jerk. That
jerk was strong enough to drag the o-ring right off the end of the bail, like
yanking a ring off the end of a pencil. The o-ring did not break. It just slid
off the end.

In short: the o-ring's grip was good enough. The hard, jammed setup created a
pull strong enough to beat that grip.

   correctly.
